Variants of A & D — Layered vs. Constellation

All 2D, left-to-right flow, looping. Variations on density, style, and how signals travel.
E — Dense Layers + Streams
Thick layered grid, flowing particle streams
More nodes per layer (12), tighter spacing. Multiple particles flow at once as continuous streams, not single dots. Feels like data flowing through a dense processor.
F — Minimal Layers + Pulse
Few layers, big nodes, pulse wave
Only 4 layers with larger nodes. Instead of particles, a pulse wave sweeps left to right — nodes light up in sequence. Clean, simple, very readable from a distance.
G — Constellation + Pathfinder
Sparse dots, signal finds its own path
Like D but with thicker trail lines and the signal "searches" for the best path through the network. Branches and sometimes dead-ends before finding the output. Exploratory feel.
H — Highway Lanes
Horizontal lanes with vertical crossings
Horizontal "highway" lines with dots at intersections. Signals travel along highways, occasionally jumping lanes vertically. Clean, modern, like a data highway diagram.
